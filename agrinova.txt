ABSTRACT
AgriNova is a mobile-based agriculture advisory application developed to address challenges in modern farming, particularly improper crop selection and difficulty in accessing nearby agricultural markets. Farmers often face reduced productivity and profit due to lack of data-driven decision-making and limited access to integrated digital tools. Traditionally, crop selection is based on past experience or informal advice, while market identification depends on manual searching or local knowledge. These conventional methods are time-consuming, less accurate, and do not provide personalized recommendations based on soil and environmental conditions. To overcome these limitations, AgriNova introduces an integrated platform that combines crop recommendation and nearest market discovery in a single system. It allows users to input soil parameters such as pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, rainfall, and season to receive suitable crop suggestions, along with the ability to locate nearby markets using geographic coordinates. The system is developed using Flutter for the mobile frontend and Django REST Framework for backend services, with SQLite as the development database. A Decision Tree algorithm is used for crop recommendation, while the Haversine formula is applied for distance calculation between locations. The application improves agricultural planning by providing accurate, data-driven recommendations and easy access to market information. It reduces dependency on guesswork and enhances productivity, efficiency, and overall decision-making for farmers.
Keywords: Crop Recommendation, Market Finder, Agriculture Advisory System, Flutter, Django REST Framework.

CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION
Introduction
Agriculture is one of the most important sectors in Nepal, contributing significantly to the country’s economy and providing employment to a large portion of the population. Despite its importance, the agricultural sector still faces numerous challenges related to productivity, planning, and market accessibility. Many farmers rely on traditional farming practices and personal experience when making decisions about crop selection, cultivation methods, and selling produce. While traditional knowledge has been valuable for generations, it often fails to adapt to changing environmental conditions, soil characteristics, and evolving market demands.
One of the major issues in modern agriculture is the lack of data-driven decision-making. Farmers frequently select crops without analyzing soil properties such as pH level, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium content, and rainfall conditions. As a result, unsuitable crops are sometimes cultivated, leading to lower yield and reduced profitability. In addition, farmers often struggle to find appropriate markets to sell their produce. The absence of reliable information about nearby agricultural markets results in delayed sales, increased transportation costs, and reduced income.
With the rapid growth of digital technology, there has been increasing interest in applying information and communication technologies (ICT) to agriculture. Digital agriculture advisory systems have emerged as a promising solution to support farmers by providing relevant information, recommendations, and services through mobile and web applications. These systems aim to enhance agricultural productivity by offering data-driven insights, improving accessibility to information, and reducing dependency on guesswork.
In the context of Nepal, the adoption of mobile-based applications has been growing due to increased smartphone usage and internet connectivity. This creates an opportunity to develop digital platforms that can assist farmers in making better agricultural decisions. However, many existing systems focus on only one aspect of farming, such as crop advisory or market information, and do not provide a comprehensive solution that integrates multiple functionalities into a single platform.
AgriNova is developed as a mobile-based agriculture advisory system to address these challenges. The system aims to provide an integrated solution that combines crop recommendation and nearest market discovery within a single application. By doing so, it helps farmers make informed decisions regarding both crop selection and market access, which are critical stages in the agricultural workflow.
The crop recommendation feature of AgriNova is designed to assist farmers in selecting suitable crops based on soil and environmental parameters. Users can input data such as pH level, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, rainfall, temperature, humidity, and season. The system processes these inputs using a Decision Tree algorithm to generate ranked crop recommendations. This approach ensures that the suggested crops are more compatible with the given soil conditions, thereby increasing the likelihood of better yield and productivity.
In addition to crop recommendation, AgriNova includes a nearest market finder feature. This module helps users locate nearby agricultural markets based on their geographical location. By using the Haversine formula, the system calculates the distance between the user’s location and available market locations stored in the database. The results are then sorted based on proximity, allowing farmers to easily identify the nearest markets for selling their produce. This feature reduces the effort required to search for markets and helps farmers make timely and efficient selling decisions.
The system is developed using modern technologies to ensure efficiency, scalability, and usability. The frontend of the application is built using Flutter, which allows the development of cross-platform mobile applications with a responsive user interface. The backend is implemented using Django REST Framework, which provides a robust environment for building secure and scalable APIs. SQLite is used as the development database to store user data, crop information, market details, and recommendation history.
AgriNova follows a client-server architecture where the mobile application interacts with the backend server through API requests. The system also implements token-based authentication and role-based authorization to ensure secure access for both users and administrators. Users can register, log in, manage their profiles, submit soil data, and access recommendations and market information, while administrators can manage system data through CRUD operations.
The development of AgriNova demonstrates the practical application of object-oriented programming principles and modern software development methodologies. The system is designed to be modular, allowing future enhancements such as integration with real-time weather data, market price updates, and advanced machine learning models for improved recommendation accuracy.
In conclusion, AgriNova represents a step towards digital transformation in agriculture by providing a user-friendly and data-driven platform for farmers. It bridges the gap between crop planning and market accessibility, which are often treated as separate processes in traditional systems. By integrating these functionalities into a single application, the system aims to improve agricultural productivity, reduce inefficiencies, and support better decision-making for farmers in Nepal.

2.1 Background Study
Digital agriculture advisory systems are technology-based platforms that support farmers in making better decisions by using data-driven approaches instead of relying only on traditional knowledge. With the increasing use of smartphones and improved internet connectivity in Nepal, mobile-based agricultural applications are becoming more accessible to farmers. These systems provide services such as crop guidance, soil-based recommendations, and market information, helping improve agricultural productivity and planning efficiency.
In the AgriNova project, a Decision Tree algorithm is used for crop recommendation. A Decision Tree is a classification method that represents decisions in a tree-like structure. It works by dividing data into smaller groups based on different conditions of input features. Each internal node represents a condition (such as soil pH or nutrient level), each branch represents the outcome of that condition, and each leaf node represents the final decision or output (recommended crop). The selection of conditions is based on measures such as entropy and information gain, which help determine the most suitable way to split the data. In this system, the algorithm processes soil parameters such as pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, rainfall, and season to determine the most appropriate crops for cultivation.
Another important component used in the system is the Haversine formula, which is applied for distance calculation between two geographical points. It calculates the shortest distance over the Earth’s surface using latitude and longitude values. This formula is commonly used in navigation and location-based services. In AgriNova, the Haversine formula is used in the nearest market finder module to calculate the distance between the user’s location and different agricultural markets stored in the system. The markets are then sorted based on distance, allowing users to easily identify the nearest ones.
These techniques are selected because they are efficient and suitable for solving the problem. The Decision Tree provides structured and condition-based crop recommendations, while the Haversine formula ensures accurate and reliable distance calculation. Together, they enable the system to deliver practical and useful agricultural advisory services.

In the AgriNova system, this algorithm is used to recommend suitable crops based on soil parameters such as pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, rainfall, and season. The system takes user input, compares it with predefined dataset conditions, and traverses the decision tree to generate the most appropriate crop recommendations.

Compare input values with trained dataset conditions.

Haversine Algorithm (Nearest Market Finder)
The Haversine formula, introduced by James Inman, is used to calculate the distance between two points on the Earth’s surface using their latitude and longitude values. It is commonly used in navigation and location-based applications.
In the AgriNova system, this formula is used to find the nearest agricultural markets based on the user’s location. The system calculates the distance between the user’s coordinates and all available market locations stored in the database, and then sorts the markets based on distance.
Steps:
Receive user latitude and longitude.
Retrieve market coordinates from the database.
Calculate distance between user and each market.
Sort markets in ascending order of distance.
Return nearest markets.

d= distance

Code Snippet (Haversine - Python):

import math

def haversine_distance(lat1, lon1, lat2, lon2):
  R = 6371.0  # Earth radius in km

  lat1_rad = math.radians(lat1)
  lon1_rad = math.radians(lon1)
  lat2_rad = math.radians(lat2)
  lon2_rad = math.radians(lon2)

  dlat = lat2_rad - lat1_rad
  dlon = lon2_rad - lon1_rad

  a = (math.sin(dlat / 2) ** 2 +
     math.cos(lat1_rad) * math.cos(lat2_rad) *
     math.sin(dlon / 2) ** 2)
  c = 2 * math.asin(math.sqrt(a))

  return round(R * c, 2)

Implementation details of Modules
1. Authentication Module
The Authentication Module handles both admin and user authentication in the AgriNova system. It supports secure registration, login, and logout for regular users, and allows administrators to access management features through token-based authentication and role-based access control.

2. Profile Management Module
The Profile Management Module supports view profile and update profile features. It allows users to manage their personal and extended profile details, such as contact information, district, address, farm size, farming experience, preferred crops, and location coordinates, which are required for personalized recommendations.

3. Crop Recommendation Module
The Crop Recommendation Module supports submit soil data and generate recommendation features. It processes soil parameters such as pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, rainfall, and season to generate ranked crop recommendations using a Decision Tree algorithm. The module also stores the recommendation result and confidence score so users can review the output later.

4. Market Finder Module
The Market Finder Module supports search nearest markets functionality. It calculates distances between user location and market locations using the Haversine formula and returns nearby markets sorted by proximity. It also helps users identify nearby agricultural markets quickly by considering geographic coordinates and optional district filtering.

 Conclusion
The AgriNova project successfully demonstrates a mobile-based agriculture advisory system tailored for farmers in Nepal. Through the implementation of an OOP-based architecture using Flutter and Django REST Framework, the system provides secure user authentication, crop recommendation based on soil data, nearest market discovery, and history tracking features. The platform bridges an important gap by integrating crop planning and market access into a single system, which is often missing in existing solutions. It also follows a modular and scalable design, making it suitable for future enhancements and real-world deployment.
 Lessons Learned/Outcomes
Through the development of the AgriNova project, valuable practical knowledge and technical skills were gained. This project provided hands-on experience in building a full-stack mobile application using Flutter for the frontend and Django REST Framework for the backend. It also enhanced understanding of object-oriented programming concepts through the design and implementation of modular components and system models using UML diagrams. Furthermore, the project helped in understanding how algorithms such as the Decision Tree and Haversine formula can be applied to solve real-world agricultural problems effectively. In addition, knowledge of software testing methodologies was improved, particularly in performing unit testing and system testing to ensure the reliability and functionality of the system.
Future Recommendations
For future enhancement, the AgriNova system can be improved by implementing production-ready deployment practices, including strict CORS policies, secure host configurations, environment-based secret management, and hardened server settings to ensure security and reliability in real-world usage. The system can also be upgraded by migrating from the current development database to a production-grade database along with scalable infrastructure to support a larger number of users. In addition, advanced advisory features can be introduced, such as explainable recommendation details, weather-aware logic, and market trend analysis to improve the accuracy and usefulness of decision-making. User engagement can be further enhanced by incorporating localized language support, notification systems, and interactive dashboards for a better user experience.
